MiniKing \- Roblox game

**Overview**  
An expansive 5000x5000 map, low-fidelity Roblox game, in which you build your own kingdom from scratch, balancing the needs of your population from food, to clothing, to security and quality-of-life. 

**Storyline**  
Your dad and mom are the King and Queen, and you live in their magnificent castle along with your two brothers and sister.  None of you and your siblings get along. You are always fighting with each other. One day, your dad (the King) is exasperated with your fighting and decides you’re all old enough to set out and build your own empires \- far away from him. 

To the child that builds the best empire after a year, he will award all his land. To help you get started, he gives you one of his top advisors to serve as your trusted guide and several horse-drawn carriages \- containing enough supplies to start your own empire.  

To create the best empire, you must build. In order to build, you need lumber, stone and gold. In order to acquire lumber, stone and gold, you need peasants to work the jobs. In order to attract peasants, you need to maintain a steady supply of essentials (food, clothing, security) \- and it doesn’t hurt to have a good Aura as their ruler. It’s up to you and your trusted advisor to find the balance between all these factors to build a prosperous kingdom.  

Beyond your city, you can explore the expansive lands and along the way discover Stone and Diamond Mines, random supplies, stashes of gold and even neighboring villages (NPC’s). If you have the Aura (and military prowess), you may try to conquer these villages; absorbing their population and stealing their gold.

**Environment:**   
5000x5000 expansive, random map of grasslands, rolling hills and lakes. 

Each map will have a couple large stone outcroppings randomly placed near opposite corners.  One outcropping will be the stone mine, where a stone quarry can be placed, and one will be the diamond mine, where the diamond quarry can be placed.  

**Player stats:** 

- **XP** (cumulative scoring):   
  - The player earns xp for every new building they create (different buildings have different XP), and for every upgrade.   
  - XP is earned for each T of resources they farm, raise or quarry.   
  - XP is earned for every peasant added to their population.  
  - XP is earned for every village they discover and every military victory  
  - Your Manor and Military upgrades require certain XP levels.   
- **Aura** (0-100 scoring): Player earns aura based on how well they support their populations. This is based on food supplies (total volume of food, plus how diverse food supply is), clothing supplies (total volume of clothes), time spent in-game (your population likes to see you), wastefulness (full storages, production imbalances), security (how well you defend against NPC neighbors), and how well you treat your NPC neighbors (attacking and killing neighbors reduces aura), while military conquests (conquering neighbors increases your aura).   
  - Aura affects your military’s hitpoints and dps (higher aura \= percentage increase in military strength)  
  - Aura affects how fast your population grows (higher aura \= how fast peasants move in to available homes)  
  - Aura affects your costs-of-goods (higher aura \= percentage decrease in production costs)  
  - Aura affects how quickly you defeat enemies (higher aura \= they surrender faster, requiring you to destroy less of their towns)

- **Peasants:**  
  - Peasants automatically move in if you provide homes, food and clothing. They will automatically fill roles in your city, starting with the most essential (farming and food production, then clothing, then resources).  If you have more peasants than jobs available, you can assign them to be trained as Spearmen, Bowmen or Knights in the military barracks.  
  - Each peasant requires a supply of food (.5T per day). If the food supply is well-rounded (bread, fruit, veggies and meat), it increases your Aura.   
  - Peasants require a supply of clothing (.1T per 5 days)  
  - Peasants requires a spot in a home  
  - If these needs aren’t met, they will start to move out, starting with military members. The last to leave will be farmers.   
- **Villagers:**  
  - As you explore, you’ll come across small to medium sized villages, which may or may not have soldiers or defensive buildings of their own.  How they react to you will largely depend on your Aura.   
    - Trading: A higher Aura will serve you well, as you will get better trade offers with a high Aura  
    - Fighting: You can attack villages with your military, but how hard they fight back and/or whether they surrender will largely depend on your Aura (high Aura \= more likely to surrender, low Aura \= fight to the death). If you win in battle \- or if they surrender \- their population will join yours and you will get their gold. A faster victory or surrender will gain you more peasants and more gold as opposed to having to fight them to the death (no peasants and minimal gold)  
    - If your Aura is low (\<50%) these villages may attack you first if you get too close, and some may even try to ransack your city. 

**Buildings:** 

- All buildings cost lumber, stone and gold to build.   
- All buildings are upgradable, which costs additional lumber, stone and gold.  
  - Upgrades increase production rates (if applicable) and storage.   
  - Upgrades affect look of the building  
- Military buildings and your Manor require certain XP and Aura levels to upgrade in addition to lumber, stone and gold.   
- The player places all buildings.   
- All buildings can be moved to new locations if room is available.  
- You can destroy any building \- except for your Manor.   
  - Destroying a building also destroys the items in its internal storage.    
  - Destroying essential buildings and not replacing them have negative consequences.    
    - Destroying houses reduces your population by the corresponding amount if not replaced after a 5 minute grace period.   
    - Destroying food-producing farms and buildings will decrease food supply. If food supply reaches 0, then your population will be reduced until equilibrium.  The first populations to leave will be soldiers, last population to leave will be wheat farmers.   
- Storage is cumulative. For example, a default wheat farm can store 30 bread, your default Manor can store an additional 60 bread, and a default Silo could store an additional 320 bread. Your supply will therefore show \[supply\]/410.  
- Production of resources is paused once their storage is at full capacity.  Paused Farms, Stables and Producers have an animated Zzzz above their building.   
- Some buildings (farms, stables, producers) require peasants as employees. If there are not enough peasants to meet the requirement, the buildings output is reduced by an equal ratio (example: Player only has 2 of the 4 required peasants, the buildings output is therefore reduced to 50%)  
- Clicking on any building gives you 3 options: Upgrade, Move, Delete  
- Clicking on a Military building gives you 5 options: Add Peasants, Set Waypoint, Upgrade, Move, Delete

  **Manor (Max 1):** This is your home, it requires 36 tiles (6x6) of space. 

- The manor stores your gold, and 1 hour of production from all buildings.   
- Storage gets filled on a first come, first serve basis, so if you have an abundance of wheat farms, they may fill your silos before other essential products.   
- Upgrades increase internal storage and hitpoints.

**Storage:**  
Silos and Warehouse increase the amount each building can store of its own product. For example, Diamond Quarry can hold 60T of diamonds. You build 2 new silos, now it can hold 6x1.2x1.2  
**Silos (Max 6\)**: Requires 4 tiles (2x2). 

- Each silo increases the amount of internal storage for each building by \+20%   
  - Upgrading a silo further increases the internal storage for each building by \+10%


**Warehouses (Max 2\)**: Requires 24 tiles (4x6). 

- Each warehouse increases the amount of internal storage for each building by \+30%   
  - Upgrading further increases the internal storage for each building by \+15%


  **MilitaryBuildings:**   
    Military buildings are unique in that they don’t have capacity limits. They train your population to become soldiers. When you have an excess population (peasants not required for farming or production), you can appoint them to a barracks to be trained. Once a peasant is trained into a soldier, they leave the building and get in formations. The player selects where soldiers gather by planting a Flag (setting a waypoint) for each type of soldier. Waypoints can be changed. Changing the Waypoint moves all soldiers, even those already in formation. Moving a military building does not move its waypoint.
